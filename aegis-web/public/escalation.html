How It Works

The Escalation Index

A composite monthly index (0–100) built from six weighted components, normalized globally and smoothed over time. The plot shows the raw and smoothed index, an alert threshold line, months where the index exceeded the threshold (red dots), pre-escalation warnings when leading indicators spike before the line crosses the threshold (orange), and a 3‑month forecast (purple dashed).

30%
Raw Conflict Intensity
Battles + explosions in absolute terms. Ensures sustained wars score high even with flat month-over-month change.
20%
Event Frequency Acceleration
Month-over-month % change in total events. Catches countries entering or re-escalating conflict before intensity peaks.
20%
Explosions / Remote Violence
Shelling, airstrikes, drone strikes, IEDs. Precede ground battle fatalities — a leading signal of escalating military operations.
15%
Strategic Developments
Troop movements, HQ changes, ceasefire collapses, territorial control shifts. Signal intent and capability changes.
10%
Civil Unrest
Protests + riots. Social instability often precedes armed conflict escalation — a leading indicator of political breakdown.
5%
Civilian Targeting Ratio
Violence against civilians as a proportion of total violent events. A shift toward civilians signals strategic deterioration.
